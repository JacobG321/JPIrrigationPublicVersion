Lawn Leveling

What is lawn leveling?

Lawn leveling and top dressing are terms that are used interchangeably. They have very similar outcomes, but over months and years look very different.

"Sand Leveling" is what most if not all golf courses do, sometimes several times per year. This is best paired with and very strongly recommended, aerating. We start by opening small holes in the soil, then begin to spread sand over the entire yard. When it gets pushed and pulled, it will fill the holes and create drainage for standing water. When brushed into the canopy level, it will sit there and fill low spots and large divets in the ground.

Screened topsoil works very similarily to sand. The difference is sand is not an organic material that can be broken down and absorbed by the plants and surrounding soil. Screened topsoil is used when the divets are to large to use sand by itself. (Our consultants will be able to elaborate more when on site)

Compost is best for top dressing. Compost uses significantly less material than sand leveling and plays a vital role in your lawns ecosystem. Since it's an organic material packed full of nutrition, it will feed the grass and also begin to amend the soil for years of rich organic soil.
